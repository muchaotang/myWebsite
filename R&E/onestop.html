MS-HCI One stop shop Research

Problem Story

Our protagonist Jeremy is a new MS-HCI student. He is enjoying his cozy life in Atlanta before he is introduced to a huge number of platforms. During his study life in Georgia Tech, he encounters a lot of problems

 

Introduction

Currently, GaTech MS-HCI students are using a multitude of online mediums to seek information, communicate and get answers to their queries. The prominent communication and information seeking mediums include social media platforms like Slack, WhatsApp, We Chat, Facebook Messenger and groups, Google hangouts as well as Email, T-Square and Forums like Piazza.

Our major Research Goals were to understand the student’s information needs, ease of access and issues with regards to prominent mediums being used for seeking information and based on these understanding of needs propose design concepts and interventions to fulfill them. Based on our objective, the following research questions were under consideration in our study:

● What are the most prominent mediums among students to access information? What are major types of information being asked for a certain medium and why?
● What are the major issues being faced by the students while seeking information through these mediums and why?
● How do differences in the difference in nationality might affect preference of a medium and their usage?

Competitive analysis

The competitive analysis overall helped us understand the strengths and weakness of prominent communication platforms. This understanding helped in us to cater for improvement in our design concept solution

Concept story

In the concept story, our protagonist Jeremy encounters the same problems. However, this time, we introduce our new product ‘Mlagic’. ‘Mlagic’ provides some significant services that can be used to facilitate him in information seeking, communication and collaboration.

 

Conclusion

-There is a real need for a more integrated platform to facilitate MS-HCI students in seeking information, communicating and collaborating.

 

-Slack for all, with need for categorization and prioritization to facilitate relevant information for MS-HCI students.

-Facilitating effective collaboration and scheduling on platforms
